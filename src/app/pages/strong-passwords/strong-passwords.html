Creating and Managing Strong Passwords

Why Strong Passwords Matter

Weak passwords are one of the easiest ways for attackers to gain access to your accounts. Strong, unique passwords for each account are your first line of defense against unauthorized access.

What Makes a Strong Password?

  • Length: At least 12-16 characters (longer is better)
  • Complexity: Mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
  • Unpredictability: Avoid common words, phrases, or patterns
  • Uniqueness: Different password for every account
  • No personal info: Don't use birthdays, names, or addresses

Avoid These Common Mistakes

  • Dictionary words (even with number substitutions like "P@ssw0rd")
  • Sequential patterns ("123456", "qwerty", "abcdef")
  • Repeated characters ("aaaaaa", "111111")
  • Personal information (birthdate, pet names, family names)
  • Reusing passwords across multiple accounts

Password Creation Tips

  • Passphrases: Use random words together (e.g., "CorrectHorseBatteryStaple")
  • Password Managers: Use tools like LastPass, 1Password, or Bitwarden to generate and store unique passwords
  • Two-Factor Authentication: Enable 2FA whenever possible for extra security
  • Regular Updates: Change passwords if you suspect a breach

Using Password Managers

Password managers securely store all your passwords in an encrypted vault, protected by one master password. They can:

  • Generate strong, random passwords for each account
  • Automatically fill in login credentials
  • Sync passwords across all your devices
  • Alert you to weak or reused passwords
  • Notify you of data breaches affecting your accounts
