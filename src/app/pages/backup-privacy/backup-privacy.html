Data Backup and Privacy Protection

Why Backup Your Data?

Regular backups protect against data loss from hardware failure, ransomware, theft, or accidental deletion. Follow the 3-2-1 rule: 3 copies of your data, on 2 different media types, with 1 copy offsite.

Backup Strategies

  • Cloud Backup: Services like Google Drive, iCloud, OneDrive, or Dropbox for automatic syncing
  • External Hard Drives: Physical backup devices kept secure and updated regularly
  • Network Attached Storage (NAS): Local network storage for home or office backups
  • Automated Backups: Set up scheduled backups to run automatically
  • Version Control: Keep multiple versions to recover from corrupted files

Privacy Protection Basics

  • Review Privacy Settings: Check settings on social media, apps, and devices regularly
  • Limit Data Sharing: Only provide necessary information to apps and services
  • Use Encryption: Encrypt sensitive files and communications
  • VPN Usage: Use a VPN on public Wi-Fi and for privacy
  • Browser Privacy: Use privacy-focused browsers or extensions that block trackers

Mobile Device Privacy

  • Review app permissions and disable unnecessary access
  • Use biometric authentication (fingerprint, face ID)
  • Enable "Find My Device" features
  • Keep your OS and apps updated
  • Be cautious with public Wi-Fi networks

Social Media Privacy

  • Make profiles private and review who can see your posts
  • Be selective about friend/connection requests
  • Don't share sensitive personal information publicly
  • Disable location tagging on posts
  • Review and remove old posts that reveal too much
  • Use strong, unique passwords for each platform

Responding to Data Breaches

  • Change passwords immediately if your account is compromised
  • Enable two-factor authentication on affected accounts
  • Monitor your accounts for suspicious activity
  • Consider credit monitoring services
  • Report identity theft to appropriate authorities
