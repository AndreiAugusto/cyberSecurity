Identifying and Avoiding Malicious Links

What Are Malicious Links?

Malicious links are URLs designed to steal information, install malware, or trick you into revealing sensitive data. They often appear in emails, text messages, social media, and fake websites.

How to Spot Malicious Links

  • Check the URL carefully: Look for misspellings or extra characters (e.g., "amaz0n.com" instead of "amazon.com")
  • Hover before clicking: On desktop, hover over links to see the actual destination URL
  • Look for HTTPS: Legitimate sites use HTTPS, especially for login or payment pages
  • Shortened URLs: Be cautious with bit.ly, tinyurl.com, etc. - use URL expanders to see the real destination
  • Suspicious senders: Unexpected emails or messages from unknown sources containing links

Common Phishing Tactics

  • Urgent security alerts claiming your account is compromised
  • Package delivery notifications when you haven't ordered anything
  • Tax refund or government benefit notifications
  • Prize or lottery winnings requiring you to click to claim
  • Invoices or receipts for purchases you didn't make

Best Practices

  • Never click links in unexpected emails or messages
  • Type URLs directly into your browser instead of clicking links
  • Use bookmarks for frequently visited websites
  • Keep your browser and security software up to date
  • When in doubt, contact the organization directly through official channels
